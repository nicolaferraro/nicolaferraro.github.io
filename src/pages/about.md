I'm a software engineer working on cloud infrastructure.

I started out at **Red Hat**, where I was a principal software engineer and got deep into Kubernetes building [Apache Camel K](https://github.com/apache/camel-k) — a platform for running [Apache Camel](https://github.com/apache/camel) integrations on Kubernetes and OpenShift. I'm a committer and PMC member on Apache Camel, and also worked on [Syndesis](https://syndesis.io) and the [Fabric8](https://github.com/fabric8io/fabric8) developer tools. Most of the posts here are from that period.

From there I moved to **Redpanda** to work on real infrastructure rather than just the apps running on top of it: four years on the Cloud platform, focused mainly on the **serverless** offering, but also on **BYOC** (Bring Your Own Cloud), where customers run Redpanda inside their own accounts while we operate it for them. I also worked on **Redpanda Connect as a service** — a connector framework for moving and transforming data that, coming from Apache Camel, felt like familiar territory with a different accent.

I've now left Redpanda and I'm joining a new startup. More to come.

You can find me on [GitHub](https://github.com/nicolaferraro) and [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/nicolaferraro/). My full CV is on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/nicolaferraro/).
